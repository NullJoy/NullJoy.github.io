My journey at IKEA Digital Hub

2021-09-05 Life Log Intership Comments Word Count: 768(words) Read Count: 4(minutes)

Last year, I worked as a full-time Software Engineer Intern at IKEA for 10 months. My job was mainly delivering frontends for the IKEA WeChat SMP. I also contributed to the static webpage library for the APP/SMP team.

Project overview

The project I mainly worked on during my internship is called IKEA WeChat SMP.

I created reusable components like address selector, broadcast popups, configurable floating buttons, advertisement card, etc. and I also designed and implemented product inventory module, optimized logic for delivery, live stream, checkout and coupons.

If you are a WeChat user, you can search for the IKEA WeChat SMP program in WeChat.

What we built is basically the online shopping center of IKEA integrated in WeChat, which is the most widely used (1.25 billion monthly active users) social app in China.

I was lucky enough to participate in the initial building work and witness the official nationwide launch of this project. This project achieved 6x,xxx average daily active users with 6xx.xK RMB average sales per day after the official launch.

A day in my life at IKEA

My work time was usually from 9:30 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Monday to Friday.

Morning routine: Stand-up meeting

Every morning when I arrived at our office building, our team would have a daily stand-up meeting to talk about our progress and check the obstacles in our work. It was usually about 10 minutes, and it marked the start of my day at work.

This was also my best chance to know about what other people in my team were working on. Gradually, I gained a more comprehensive understanding of the projects’ status in my team as well.

Sprint start: getting my tasks on JIRA

Since I worked in a Scrum team, we had sprints. A sprint is like an iteration period. A normal sprint was 2 weeks usually. When the sprint started, we would have an Iteration (or Sprint) Planning Meeting (IPM). In this meeting, we would discuss how much of the backlog could be committed to and delivered in this sprint. The tasks would be evaluated in story points.

After the sprint started, I would have several tasks on JIRA which showed the work I needed to get done in this sprint. I was also responsible to update my task status every day.

Sprint ongoing: develop, discuss, and deliver

During the sprint, I would discuss some ambiguous points with the Product Owner and code to meet the expectations. This process also included a lot of conversations with the UI/UX designer, the Quality Assurance Engineer, the Backend Development Engineer, and even people from other teams or departments.

At first I was super shy and it was a big challenge for me to gather the courage to start conversations with so many people. It was because of how nice the people here were that made me comfortable to express my opinion and become more and more confident.

When I thought my code was ready, I would inform a QA Engineer to put my code into testing. When the testings were passed, and the PO thought it fulfilled the acceptance criteria, my code would go online to our shopping program for people to use.

Snacks were amazing!!

Here we had different kinds of snacks and fruit every day. I loved the cheesecakes - I know it’s not that healthy - which were so beautiful and delicious. The kiwis here were always sweeter than the ones I brought from the market. And when there was an activity or party, we had more. There were yogurt, bread, chocolate cake, fried chicken, meatballs, sausages, cookies, sushis, more than ten kinds of drinks…

Summary

2020 was definitely a special year for many people. For me as well.

Instead of pursuing my graduate study right after finishing my undergrad study as I originally planned, I was “forced” by COVID-19 to take a gap year and did my first formal internship in life. I was lucky enough to spend that year with people at IKEA.

I wanted to thank everyone in my team, who was the kindest person and most willing to answer my questions at any time. I also wanted to especially thank my line manager Leo and my mentor, Ciki, who perfected my internship.

When I first arrived at IKEA, I almost knew nothing about real-industry software development. Ciki was always happy to give me useful instructions and show me the right direction to work in.

It was a great time working here, and the people here were so welcoming and so supportive to me.

See you, IKEA!
